3D Print Farm

BUS Core gives multi-printer operations a structured way to track filament and resin, record print batches, and calculate actual cost per print — without spreadsheets or cloud services.

Common Problems

  • Filament and resin inventory across multiple spools, bottles, and printers tracked informally
  • No structured record of which materials went into which print batch
  • Cost per print is a guess or a retroactive spreadsheet calculation
  • Scaling from one printer to several breaks informal tracking
  • Production history lives in slicer logs or memory, not a usable ledger

Where BUS Core Fits

  • Track filament and resin inventory by type, color, and quantity
  • Create production blueprints that define material requirements per product
  • Record manufacturing runs tying specific material consumption to finished output
  • Calculate actual cost per print including material usage
  • Maintain a production ledger across your entire farm

Practical Fit Checklist

  • You run multiple printers producing physical products
  • You manage inventory across filament types or resin bottles
  • You need to know what a print actually costs to produce
  • You want a structured production history, not a spreadsheet
  • You prefer a local-first tool that runs on your own machine
